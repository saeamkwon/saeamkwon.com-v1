About Quad

Quad is an edtech startup that aims to help students keep track of their assignments so that they can worry less and enjoy their college experience. Everything is crowdsourced so students are able to help each other and doublecheck their peers.

Overview

The problem:

The initial style of Quad was sleek and sophisticated. Individual flows were dark themed which made it obvious to the user that they were in a flow to complete a specific action.

The Solution:

The target audience of college students were more open to light and approachable designs. The style guide was changed to have a lighter palette with colorful illustrations to appeal to .

An Approachable Style

Quad's initial style guide had the app go for a sleek and sophisticated design. But since their target audience is college students, the direction was taken into a more approachable design with a light base and colorful illustrations.

Onboarding

Since onboarding is the first impression of the app, the style had to be very transparent. Instead of presenting the entire screen for the features, very minimal components are shown for fast comprehension and clean design.

Illustrations

During the sign up process, illustrations of students and the mascot, Evan. The students give the users a sense of connection and makes them comfortable during the sign up process. Evan was created as a mascot to create a friendly atmosphere.

Another illustration that was added to the process was illustrations of campuses. School pride is a very universal feeling. I took the most iconic buildings from the campuses Quad was going to launch at.

Initial Designs

Quad's initial style guide was sleek and sophisticated so a lot of the flows were dark themed. This is the flow for adding an assignment onto Quad.

Long Forms and Inputs

One of the main issues with this flow was that at first, the form seems simple. Four inputs. However, after filling out a part, the form gains two additional inputs. Additional inputs equals more clicks which equals longer forms.

Dark to Light

With the new direction in style, the flows were changed into lighter themes and made simpler into a clean and light blue interface.

Responsive Forms

To tackle the perception of the long process, the form was first condensed into 3 input fields. Then the Due Date input leads to an entirely new form hence the arrow present on the right side.

This then leads to a responsive form that will present or hide steps depending on your previous inputs. This eliminates the number of clicks by half and makes the process shorter.
